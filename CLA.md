# Contributor License Agreement

All external contributors must have a signed Contributor License Agreement
(CLA) on file before their contributions can be merged.

This project uses a CLA from day 1 because it is dual-licensed:

- community releases are available under AGPL-3.0-or-later;
- commercial licenses may be issued separately by the project owner.

## Summary

By contributing, you keep ownership of your contribution, but you grant the
project owner the rights needed to use, modify, distribute, sublicense, and
relicense your contribution as part of Open Agentic Threat Defense under both
open-source and commercial licenses.

## Required Grants

Each contributor grants the project owner:

- a worldwide, royalty-free, perpetual, irrevocable copyright license to use,
  reproduce, modify, prepare derivative works of, publicly perform, publicly
  display, sublicense, and distribute the contribution;
- permission to license the contribution under AGPL-3.0-or-later and separate
  commercial licenses;
- a patent license for any patent claims necessarily infringed by the
  contribution.

## Contributor Promises

Each contributor confirms that:

- they have the right to submit the contribution;
- their employer or organization has authorized the contribution if required;
- the contribution is their original work or is submitted with compatible
  permissions;
- they will not knowingly submit code that violates third-party rights.

## No Warranty

Contributions are provided "as is", without warranty or liability.

## Signing Process

Until an automated CLA bot is configured, maintainers must record CLA approval
manually before merging external contributions.

Required record fields:

- contributor legal name;
- GitHub username or commit email;
- individual or company contributor status;
- date accepted;
- accepting maintainer;
- CLA version.

This document is an operational starting point, not legal advice. Before
accepting third-party contributions at scale, have counsel review the final CLA
language and signing workflow.
